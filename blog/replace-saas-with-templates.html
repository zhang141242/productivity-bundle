When SaaS Still Makes Sense

I'm not anti-SaaS. There are situations where paying monthly makes sense:

  • Team collaboration at scale — If 20+ people need to access and edit data simultaneously, Excel isn't the right tool.
  • Compliance requirements — If you need SOC 2, GDPR audit trails, or HIPAA compliance, SaaS tools come with those built in.
  • API integrations — If your CRM needs to talk to your email tool, which talks to your accounting software, stick with SaaS.
  • You genuinely don't have 10 minutes a week — If manual data entry would literally cost you more than the subscription, pay the subscription.

But for the vast majority of solopreneurs, freelancers, and micro-businesses? The SaaS stack is a luxury tax on not knowing Excel has templates.

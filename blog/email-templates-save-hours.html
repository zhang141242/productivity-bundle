10 Email Templates That Save 5+ Hours Per Week

Published: June 2026 · 6 min read

The average business owner sends 30-40 work emails per week. At 5 minutes each, that's 3+ hours just writing emails. But here's the real cost: most of those emails are variations of the same 10 messages you send over and over.

Template them once. Save hours every week. Forever.

The Math

Without templates: 35 emails/week × 5 min = 175 minutes (2.9 hours)
With templates: 35 emails/week × 1 min (customize + send) = 35 minutes
Time saved: 140 minutes/week = 120+ hours per year

At $50/hour, that's $6,000 of your time back. Every year.

The 10 Emails You Should Template Today

1. The Introductory Email (After Someone Signs Up)

When to use: New newsletter subscriber, new customer, new lead magnet download.

Subject: Welcome to [Company] — here's what to do next

Hi [First Name],

Thanks for signing up. You made a great decision. Here are 3 things to do next:
1. [Action item 1]
2. [Action item 2]
3. [Action item 3]

If you have any questions, just reply to this email. I read every one.

[Your Name]

2. The Follow-Up (After No Response)

When to use: 3-5 days after your first email got no reply.

Subject: Re: [Original Subject]

Hi [First Name],

Following up on this — I know you're busy. Any thoughts?

If the timing isn't right, no worries at all. Just let me know either way.

Best,
[Your Name]

3. The Proposal Email

When to use: Sending a quote, proposal, or estimate to a potential client.

Subject: Proposal for [Project Name] — [Your Company]

Hi [First Name],

As discussed, here's the proposal for [project].

Key points:
• Scope: [1-2 sentence scope]
• Timeline: [Start date] to [End date]
• Investment: $[Amount]

Full details are in the attached proposal. If it looks good, I can start on [date].

Happy to hop on a call if you have questions.

[Your Name]

4. The Invoice Email

When to use: Every time you send an invoice (which should be automated, but if not... template it).

Subject: Invoice #[Number] from [Your Company]

Hi [First Name],

Please find invoice #[Number] for [service/product] attached.

Amount: $[Amount]
Due date: [Date]
Payment method: [Link/method]

Thank you for your business!

[Your Name]

5. The "Checking In" Email (Existing Clients)

When to use: Monthly or quarterly touch-base with ongoing clients.

Subject: Quick check-in — how's [Project/Product] going?

Hi [First Name],

Just checking in — how are things going with [project/product]?

Anything I can help with? Any questions or feedback?

[Optional: mention a new feature or relevant tip]

[Your Name]

6. The Testimonial Request

When to use: 1-2 weeks after a successful project or purchase.

Subject: Quick favor? (30 seconds)

Hi [First Name],

I'm glad [project/product] worked out well for you!

If you have 30 seconds, would you mind leaving a quick review? It helps me a lot and I really appreciate it.

[Link to review page]

No pressure if you're busy — just wanted to ask!

Thanks,
[Your Name]

7. The "No" Email (Saying No Gracefully)

When to use: Turning down a project, feature request, or partnership that's not a fit.

Subject: Re: [Original Subject]

Hi [First Name],

Thanks for thinking of me for this. After reviewing [the details], I don't think I'm the right fit at this time.

[Optional: recommend someone else or explain briefly why]

I appreciate you reaching out, and I wish you the best with [project].

[Your Name]

8. The Deadline Extension Request

When to use: You need more time. It happens.

Subject: [Project Name] — Timeline Update

Hi [First Name],

Quick update: I need until [new date] to deliver [deliverable] instead of [original date].

Reason: [1 sentence explanation — be specific, not vague]

Here's what's done so far: [list progress made — shows you've been working]

I'm confident the new date is realistic. Apologies for the shift.

[Your Name]

9. The Out-of-Office Auto-Responder

When to use: Vacation, conference, or any time you won't respond within 24 hours.

Subject: Out of office: [Date range]

Hi,

I'm out of the office from [start date] to [end date] with limited email access.

• For urgent matters: Contact [colleague name] at [email]
• For [specific topic]: Check [link/resource]
• For everything else: I'll respond when I'm back on [return date]

[Your Name]

10. The Re-engagement Email

When to use: A customer or lead went cold. Win them back.

Subject: Still interested in [Product/Service]?

Hi [First Name],

It's been a little while. I wanted to check if you're still interested in [product/service].

Since we last spoke, [mention one update: new feature, case study, price change, etc.].

If you're still interested, I'd love to pick up where we left off. If not, no worries — just let me know and I'll stop emailing.

[Your Name]

How to Actually Use These

  1. Copy the template into your email tool (Gmail, Outlook, whatever you use)
  2. Customize the bracketed parts with your specific details
  3. Save as a canned response/template in your email client:
    • Gmail: Settings → Advanced → Templates → Enable
    • Outlook: Home → New Items → More Items → Save as Quick Part
    • Apple Mail: Create a signature for each, switch between them
  4. Use for 1 week. After 5 days you'll wonder why you didn't do this years ago.

Pro tip: Keep a Google Doc with all 10 templates. When you update one (better subject line, clearer wording), update the master doc. It becomes your team's email playbook.

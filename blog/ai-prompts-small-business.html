50 AI Prompts Every Small Business Owner Should Save

Published: June 2026

Small business owners wear 15 hats. Marketing. Sales. Customer service. Product development. Accounting. The list never ends.

AI can't run your business for you, but it can handle the time-consuming writing, analysis, and planning tasks that eat your evenings and weekends. Here are the prompts.

Marketing & Content

You are a copywriter for [INDUSTRY]. Write a 200-word product description for [PRODUCT]. Highlight 3 key benefits. Use a [TONE] tone. Include a call to action.
Generate 10 social media post ideas for a [BUSINESS TYPE]. Each post should include: hook, body (2 sentences), and 3 hashtags. Focus on [THEME: education / behind-the-scenes / customer stories].
Write a Google Ads headline and description for [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Headline: 30 chars max. Description: 90 chars max. Include the keyword [KEYWORD]. Focus on [UNIQUE SELLING POINT].

Customer Communication

Write a response to a customer who complained about [ISSUE]. Acknowledge their frustration. Explain what went wrong honestly. Tell them how we fixed it. Offer [COMPENSATION] as goodwill. Keep it warm and human — no corporate speak.
Write a follow-up email to a customer who made a purchase 2 weeks ago. Ask if they're happy. Offer help if needed. Gently suggest they leave a review. Keep under 100 words.

Operations & Planning

Create a 90-day action plan for launching [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Break it into: Month 1 (Foundation), Month 2 (Build), Month 3 (Launch). Each month: 3 key objectives, 5 action items per objective. Include dependencies and risks.
Analyze this pricing strategy for [PRODUCT]: [DESCRIBE CURRENT PRICING]. Compare with 3 competitors. Recommend whether we should raise, lower, or restructure. Explain the reasoning with unit economics.
